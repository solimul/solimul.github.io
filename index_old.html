Welcome to my profile page. I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the School of Computer Science (SCS) at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU). My postdoc supervisor is associate professor Marijn Heule. I completed my PhD and MSc from the computing science department of University of Alberta, Canada. I also have 5 years of software development experience from Canada.
My research interests lie in the area of automated reasoning (AR). AR studies computational frameworks that mimic reasoning aspects of human cognition. Currently, my focus is on boolean Satisfiability solving (SAT) solving, an important paradigm in AR. SAT solving algorithms generate proofs for syntactically valid statements in propositional logic. In recent years, SAT solving has found numerous important practical applications in areas, such as formal verification, theorem proving, and AI.
I am interested in developing novel and efficient algorithms for SAT. In particular, I am focusing on the following issues:
  • Derive empirical insights on (i) the innerworking of the decision heuristics of Conflict Driven Clause Learning (CDCL) SAT solvers, and (ii) the conflict generation pattern in CDCL. Then improve CDCL based on these insghts.
  • CDCL SAT solvers scale well on some structured problems, such as problems from verification domain. However, there are problems from other domains, such as encryption, which are challenging for CDCL. I am interested in developing an improved understanding on this varying performance behavior of CDCL. What makes problems from domain H (resp. E) hard (resp. easy) for CDCL to solve?
  • Develop decision heuristics for newer proof paradigms in SAT, such as Satisfaction Driven Clause Learning (SDCL).
  • Develop better understanding on the interaction of machine learned knowledge and SAT search to improve SAT.
  • Find new usability of SAT solvers by extending standard SAT solving frameworks and by developing new applications.
My postdoc position at CMU is partially supported by an NSERC Postdoctoral Fellowship from Canada.
